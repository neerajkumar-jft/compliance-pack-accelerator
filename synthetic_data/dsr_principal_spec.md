## Why this principal specifically

The choice of iteration 4217 is not random. Reasoning:
- **Mid-range ID** — not the first customer (which is often special-cased), not the last
- **High-overlap** — index positioned to land in the customer/user overlap subset per seed 42
- **Realistic transaction count** — Zipf distribution at this index gives a moderate transaction count (not 0, not 500), which makes the demo narrative believable
- **Includes a prior withdrawal** — the seed deliberately places an earlier withdrawal for `marketing_email`, which makes the audit trail more interesting to walk through
- **No retention conflict on the primary data** — customer and user records have no retention obligation, so erasure is immediate and can be demonstrated live; only transactions land in the residual bucket

If a future version of the generator changes the seed or the distribution logic, `customer_04217`'s footprint may shift. The manifest captures the authoritative expected state; tests assert against manifest, not against hardcoded constants.
